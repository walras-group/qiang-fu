Research Interest

Optimization & Sampling

My research lies broadly in optimization, statistics, and machine learning. I am particularly focused on developing provably efficient algorithms for optimization and sampling to improve the training and inference of modern machine learning models — with a special interest in the elegant geometry of Hamiltonian dynamics and its role in bridging continuous-time flows, accelerated optimization, and probabilistic sampling.

Research Experience

2024 — Present

Research Assistant

Yale University, Department of Computer Science — advised by Prof. Andre Wibisono

Researching non-Gaussian priors in flow-based generative models (flow matching, rectified flow, stochastic interpolants); designing novel LLM pretraining optimizers based on Hamiltonian flows; advancing the theory of accelerated convex optimization via Hamiltonian dynamics.

2022 — 2024

Research Student

MIT, Department of EECS — advised by Prof. Ashia Wilson

Developed provably convergent algorithms for training two-layer neural networks via spacetime discretization of mean-field underdamped Langevin dynamics (ICML 2024); designed new accelerated stochastic optimization algorithms for quasar-convex functions with applications to linear dynamical systems and generalized linear models (ICML 2023).

2021 — 2022

Research Student

Sun Yat-sen University, School of Mathematics

Collaborated on fractional Laplacian-based image inpainting methods, developing theoretical guarantees and numerical algorithms. Published in Inverse Problems and Imaging.
